Hop the Shortcut!

Oops, Am I killing all the grass?
Nope, I am just playing Hopscotch!

Change your everyday informal shortcut into a playful hopscotch game.

01

Protocol of the Site

Site

The site is a patch of ground in front of N10. It sits between the brick path and the entrance area, along the route people use when heading toward the convenience store.

Protocol

Although a brick road already exists, people often walk across the grass because it is faster. Over time, repeated footsteps erased the grass and produced an unofficial path.

Official protocol

Follow the brick road.

Actual protocol

Take the shortcut.

02

Crafting Process

The first prototype confirmed that the hopscotch pattern could change the way the shortcut was perceived. However, the material was too weak and unstable for the actual ground. The final version was made stronger, clearer, and more visible.

Prototype

The logic worked; the material failed.

The prototype was light, fragile, and difficult to fix onto the stiff ground. This failure clarified what the final installation needed: stronger material, clearer visibility, and better weight.

Final Making

Building a stronger playable path.

The final pieces were prepared to be more stable and visually legible. They were placed directly over the worn shortcut, following the direction people already used.
